Origin

Hot Wheels, a collection,
and an honest choice.

I didn't have a childhood story of taking apart televisions or building machines in the garage. My story starts with a collection of Hot Wheels — die-cast cars lined up with the seriousness only a child can give small things. Each one chosen, named, remembered. My parents saw that love and fed it. My father, who never shared the obsession, showed up for it anyway — and in doing so, turned a collection into a bond.

When it came to choosing a career, I was honest with myself: I was good at maths and exact sciences. I chose Automotive Systems Engineering at IPN for practical reasons — the kind of reasons a first-generation professional from a hardworking family understands without needing to explain.

What happened next is what I'm most proud of: I fell in love with the work. Not because someone told me I would. But because I committed, and passion followed commitment. That sequence — commit first, let the love come — has become a guiding principle in everything I do.

Foundations

The people who made me who I am.

From my mother
Emotional intelligence & logic
The pursuit of rationality paired with the capacity to feel it. She taught me that empathy and precision are not opposites.
From my father
Courage & technical curiosity
A leader of 7 siblings, always the one who stood up. He gave me the willingness to go first, the bravery to be accountable, and the care to bring others along. His pursuit of growth moved our whole family across the country — and that restlessness lives in me too.
From my brother
Vulnerability & stubbornness
The ability to be afraid and go anyway. Some distances between people are temporary — the values they leave behind are not.
From the road
The art of listening
Moving city to city as a child, I didn't always have the words to make friends quickly. So I learned to listen first — to understand before speaking. Every leadership lesson I've ever given started here.

And from the person who, for ten years, held up a mirror and showed me who I was capable of becoming — Some chapters end so the next one can begin.

Outside the Office

Tennis — The Sport of My Life

Picked up a racket in Mérida around mid-elementary school and never truly put it down. At its peak, 2–4 hours a day, every day — the only sport I've ever wanted to repeat. I've tried football, basketball, volleyball, athletics, swimming. Tennis is the one I still watch on television even when I don't follow the season. I've coached beginner and intermediate players at university. Right now: occasional matches. Always: the same love for the game.

Hot Wheels — A Serious Collection

Started as a child's obsession, became a lifelong archive. Each car chosen with intention — every piece a small, precise thing that held meaning before I had words for it. It's the most honest version of my origin story: I loved cars before I understood them, and that love came first as something you could hold in your hand.

Baking — Canolis & Family Tradition

My father's family carries a tradition of bread-making. I grew up loving bread in every form — sweet, salty, elaborate, simple. When Christmas trips to Ciudad Altamirano ended as my grandparents passed, we started our own family dinner at home. That first year, I made Canolis. I am a critic about bread, which means I hold my own work to the same standard. They've been repeated only a few times since — which means they stay special.

Travel — A Lifelong Practice

I didn't choose to travel — I was raised doing it. Mexico end to end as a child. Europe before I was a teenager, made possible by my parents' years of careful effort. Back to Europe two years ago. Multiple visits to the US and Canada for work and pleasure. Travel, for me, is less about destinations and more about the recalibration it forces. Every new place shows you what you assumed was universal is actually just local.

What Drives Me

"Next-generation vehicles are not about technology. They are about delivering experiences, stories, and life."

I work in a field where thousand approvals, backup data, and documentation layers can make you forget why you started. I haven't forgotten. The reason is the person who opens the door, gets in, and drives — and whether what we built gave them something worth feeling.

My passion for troubleshooting and issue resolution isn't about closing tickets. It's about the moment a customer stops experiencing a problem they didn't know we caused. That gap between failure and resolution — that's where the work lives, and where I want to keep working.

The next step is a Master's in Vehicle Engineering in Sweden — not to become more technical in isolation, but to deepen the foundation that connects systems thinking to the future of how people move through the world.
